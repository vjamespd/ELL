Process Timeline

1. Donor Screening

Rigorous multi-tiered screening of healthy, consenting donors. Includes comprehensive medical and social history review, physical assessment, and extensive infectious disease testing beyond standard requirements.

2. Pathogen Testing

CLIA-certified laboratory testing for HIV I/II, HCV, HBV, Syphilis, and other pathogens using Nucleic Acid Testing (NAT) to ensure absolute tissue safety before processing begins.

3. Processing & Isolation

Conducted in ISO Class 5 cleanrooms. Proprietary minimal manipulation techniques are used to isolate target cells while maintaining their structural and functional integrity, ensuring high viability counts.

4. Cryopreservation & Storage

Controlled-rate freezing using optimal cryoprotectants. Products are stored in vapor-phase liquid nitrogen (-196°C) within continuously monitored, redundant-alarmed storage facilities.
